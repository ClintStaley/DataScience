  1. In line 7 of Algorithm 18.1 (p. 471) give an example of a 2-attribute, 2-class data set for which Σ1\Sigma_1Σ1​ might be nearly diagonal but Σ2\Sigma_2Σ2​ is not. Explain your answer.

Attributes height and width, for classes square and rectangle. Diagonal Σ\SigmaΣ indicates independent distribution within the class, which would be true for rectangle, but not for square

  1. Explain the term ∏j=1dmj\prod_{j=1}^d m_j∏j=1d​mj​ in equation 18.8:

    • What do the mjm_jmj​ values signify?
    • Why would we multiply them all together?
    • What does the product signify?

mjm_jmj​ is the number of categorical choices for attribute j. We are supplying one "free" count of each possible mixture of categorical choices for d attributes. The number of such choices is the product of choices for category 1, times that for category 2, etc.

  1. Give an example of a classification problem where naive Bayes might be expected to fail. Explain your answer.

The answer to question 3, since it is the very correlation of height and width that marks a square. Multiplying independent height and width likelihood would not distinguish squares from rectangles.
