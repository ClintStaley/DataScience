You divide one of the existing events into two equally-likely events, e.g. dividing an event E with 20% probability into E1 and E2 each with 10% probability.   2. Entropy is a common means of measuring password strength. A very good password will have, for instance, 40 bits of entropy. Describe a password choosing system that would result in that much entropy.

  3. A very good means of choosing high-strength password is to randomly build a phrase by drawing words from a dictionary of say 4096 possibilities. A four-word such phrase might be "red dog true red". Assumingly uniformly random choices from a 4096 word dictionary, what is the information entropy of such a pass phrase? Why? Is it as random, for instance, as a safe password of 7 2-digit numbers, e.g. 01-00-20-98-43-28-42? Why or why not?
