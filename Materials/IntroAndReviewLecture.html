    • Statistical sampling

      • A statistic is some numerical measure of a sample of a distribution
        • Can be mathematically identical to measures like mean, variance, but maintain the distinction -- it's on a sample, without assuming the underlying distribution.
        • A statistic is its own random variable. A statistic that is the mean of n samples has its own mean, variance, etc.
      • Empirical PMF. Probability distribution as determined by the sample.
        • Imagine you don't even know the underlying PMF
      • Consider the statistic of a sum of n independent samples: ∑1nxi\sum_1^nx_i∑1n​xi​
        • Assume we do n samples, and compute the sum, over and over, to get many sample sums.
        • What is the mean of such a statistic? nμn\munμ, for obvious intuitive reasons.
        • Can also be geometrically "seen" as center of mass of the sample points.
      • Expected variance of n-sample-sum is more complex.
        • Sum is on average n times a sample size, so do we expect n-squared the variance? Not necessarily because mean of a set of n-samples won't actually be μ\muμ
        • Essential point is that E(((∑1nxi)−nμ)2)=E((∑1nxi−μ)2) =E(n(xi−μ)2)=nE((xi−μ)2)=nσ2E(((\sum_1^nx_i)-n\mu)^2) = E((\sum_1^nx_i-\mu)^2)\ = E(n(x_i-\mu)^2) = nE((x_i-\mu)^2) = n\sigma^2E(((∑1n​xi​)−nμ)2)=E((∑1n​xi​−μ)2) =E(n(xi​−μ)2)=nE((xi​−μ)2)=nσ2
        • We can replace the summation with an n-factor because E((x1−μ)+(x2−μ)+...+(xn−μ))2=E((x1−μ)2+(x2−μ)2+...+(xn−μ)2+(x1μ)(x2−μE((x_1-\mu)+(x_2-\mu)+...+(x_n-\mu))^2 = E((x_1 - \mu)^2 + (x_2-\mu)^2 + ... + (x_n-\mu)^2 + (x_1\mu)(x_2-\muE((x1​−μ)+(x2​−μ)+...+(xn​−μ))2=E((x1​−μ)2+(x2​−μ)2+...+(xn​−μ)2+(x1​μ)(x2​−μ + other mixed terms)
        • For any mixed term E(xi−μ)(xj−μ)=0E(x_i-\mu)(x_j-\mu)= 0E(xi​−μ)(xj​−μ)=0
          • Assuming what condition, and why? Independence, so that each term will be negative/positive at same rate.
      • OK, so variance of a sample-sum grows with n, but not as n2n^2n2, because only "aligned" terms of the summation actually contribute to the variance.

    • Now, what about n-sample-average: 1n∑1nxi\frac{1}{n}\sum_1^nx_in1​∑1n​xi​ What is this statistic's mean? μ\muμ, obviously

    • And its variance?

      • How does it relate to the variance of the sample-sum? Each term in the sum is multiplied by 1n2\frac{1}{n^2}n21​
      • So, variance is nσ2n2=σ2n\frac{n\sigma^2}{n^2} = \frac{\sigma^2}{n}n2nσ2​=nσ2​

    • Variance of n-sample-average reduces by 1/n, and stddev by 1n\frac{1}{\sqrt n}n​1​

      • Is sample average actually the same as μ\muμ every time? **No, its expected value is μ\muμ but it has a variance equal to σ2/n\sigma^2/nσ2/n
      • So is n-sample-average variance (squared difference from sample average) different from actual variance? Yes, we'd intuitively expect the sample average to reduce sample variance by a factor of 1/n
